\begin{center}
\textbf{Abstract}
\end{center}
\baselineskip=.8\baselineskip

The mismatch between general-domain training speech and real telephone or Voice over IP (VoIP) audio remains a major obstacle to robust Persian automatic speech recognition. This thesis develops and evaluates a reproducible approach for adapting Whisper-small to such conditions. An aligned clean--degraded data pipeline applies environmental noise, gain variation, bandwidth restriction, real FFmpeg codec round-trips, and packet-loss simulation while recording per-example seeds and degradation metadata in JSONL manifests. Whisper-small is then fine-tuned either on ordinary Persian data or on a multi-condition mixture of ordinary and degraded speech.

The thesis also evaluates a dual-view model that derives original and enhanced log-Mel representations from the same degraded input. The enhancer is a residual U-Net with temporal modeling at its bottleneck. Both views pass through a shared Whisper encoder and are combined using bidirectional cross-attention and a learned time--feature gate. Training proceeds in three stages: enhancer warm-up, fusion training with the Whisper backbone frozen, and joint end-to-end optimization.

Evaluation on five Persian test sets, including real telephone and VoIP speech from AGFarsdat, shows that multi-condition training is the strongest method overall. Aggregate word error rate falls from 23.27\% for ordinary Whisper fine-tuning to 17.83\%. The fusion model reaches 18.44\%, improving on ordinary Whisper but not outperforming the multi-condition baseline; its 14.04\% character error rate is also worse than both single-stream systems. A mean gate weight of 0.9961 on the original view indicates collapse toward the noisy/original stream. Fusion attains the lowest numerical WER on AGFarsdat, but its 0.42 percentage-point margin over the multi-condition model is too small to claim a stable advantage without statistical testing. The central result is therefore that traceable telecom-oriented augmentation and multi-condition training are currently more effective than adding a complex enhancement-and-fusion path.

\bigskip\noindent\textbf{Keywords}:
Persian automatic speech recognition, telephone speech, Voice over IP, Whisper, multi-condition training, speech enhancement, dual-view fusion
